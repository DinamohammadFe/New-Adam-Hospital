What is Egg Freezing?

Egg freezing, also known as oocyte cryopreservation, is a process where a woman's eggs are extracted, frozen, and stored for later use. This fertility preservation technique allows women to maintain their reproductive options by preserving eggs at their current age and quality. The frozen eggs can be thawed, fertilized, and transferred to the uterus when the woman is ready to conceive.

Benefits of Egg Freezing

Egg freezing provides women with reproductive autonomy and peace of mind. It offers the flexibility to pursue personal and professional goals while preserving fertility potential for the future.

PRESERVING FERTILITY AT PEAK QUALITY
+

Egg quality naturally declines with age, particularly after 35. By freezing your eggs at a younger age, you preserve them at their current quality, maintaining better chances of successful pregnancy in the future.

This gives you the opportunity to use younger, healthier eggs even if you decide to have children later in life, significantly improving your chances of conception and reducing the risk of genetic abnormalities.

CAREER AND LIFE PLANNING FLEXIBILITY
+

Egg freezing allows you to pursue your career goals, education, or personal aspirations without the pressure of a biological clock. You can focus on establishing your career, finding the right partner, or achieving financial stability.

This reproductive autonomy empowers women to make family planning decisions on their own timeline, reducing stress and anxiety about age-related fertility decline.

MEDICAL FERTILITY PRESERVATION
+

For women facing medical treatments that may affect fertility, such as chemotherapy or radiation, egg freezing offers hope for future biological children. It's also beneficial for those with conditions like endometriosis or PCOS.

At Adam, we work closely with oncologists and other specialists to provide timely fertility preservation options, ensuring you have the best chance of preserving your reproductive potential before treatment begins.

Did You Know?

Women are born with all the eggs they'll ever have, and egg quality begins to decline in the late 20s, with a more significant decline after age 35? Freezing your eggs in your 20s or early 30s can significantly improve your future fertility outcomes?

Egg Freezing Step by Step

Your egg freezing journey is carefully planned and executed with precision. Here's what the process typically involves, so you can feel confident about preserving your fertility options.

Initial Consultation

Meet with our fertility specialist to discuss your goals, medical history, and assess your ovarian reserve. We'll create a personalized egg freezing plan tailored to your specific needs and timeline.

Ovarian Stimulation

You'll take hormone medications for 8-14 days to stimulate your ovaries to produce multiple eggs. Regular monitoring with blood tests and ultrasounds ensures optimal response and timing.

Egg Retrieval

A minimally invasive procedure performed under light sedation to collect mature eggs from your ovaries. The procedure takes about 20-30 minutes and you can go home the same day.

Egg Assessment

Our embryologists carefully evaluate each egg for maturity and quality. Only mature, high-quality eggs are selected for freezing to maximize future success rates.

Vitrification Process

Using advanced vitrification technology, eggs are rapidly frozen to -196Â°C in liquid nitrogen. This ultra-fast freezing prevents ice crystal formation and preserves cellular integrity.

Secure Storage

Your frozen eggs are stored in our state-of-the-art cryopreservation facility with 24/7 monitoring, backup systems, and comprehensive security measures to ensure their safety.

Future Use

When you're ready to use your frozen eggs, they'll be carefully thawed, fertilized with sperm, and the resulting embryos transferred to your uterus. Our high survival rates give you the best chance of achieving pregnancy when the time is right for you.

Success Rates with Frozen Eggs

Age-Dependent Success

Success rates depend primarily on the age at which eggs were frozen. Eggs frozen before age 35 have the highest success rates, with live birth rates of 30-60% per egg retrieval cycle.

Quality Preservation

Our advanced vitrification technology ensures over 90% egg survival rate after thawing, maintaining the same genetic potential as fresh eggs from the time of freezing.

Factors Affecting Success Rates

Success rates depend on various factors including age at egg freezing, number of eggs retrieved and frozen, egg quality, and overall reproductive health. Our experienced team will discuss your individual prognosis during consultation.
