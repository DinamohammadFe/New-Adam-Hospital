What is IVF?

In vitro fertilization (IVF) is a procedure designed to improve fertility in patients who have not been successful with ovulation induction or intrauterine insemination, or who are not candidates for those more conservative treatments. Eggs are surgically removed from the ovaries and fertilized with sperm in a laboratory environment. The fertilized egg, i.e. embryo, is then transferred into the uterus.

When is IVF Recommended?

  • ✓ Blocked or damaged fallopian tubes
  • ✓ Low sperm count or motility
  • ✓ Unexplained infertility
  • ✓ Endometriosis-related infertility
  • ✓ Advanced maternal age
  • ✓ Ovulation disorders
  • ✓ Genetic conditions
  • ✓ Fertility preservation

IVF Treatment Process

Initial Consultation & Assessment

Comprehensive evaluation of your medical history, fertility tests, and personalized treatment planning with our experienced fertility specialists.

Ovarian Stimulation

Hormone medications to stimulate your ovaries to produce multiple eggs, with regular monitoring through ultrasounds and blood tests.

Egg Retrieval

Minor surgical procedure performed under sedation to collect mature eggs from your ovaries using ultrasound guidance.

Fertilization & Embryo Development

Eggs are fertilized with sperm in our advanced laboratory, and embryos are carefully monitored for 3-5 days as they develop.

Embryo Transfer

The best quality embryo(s) are carefully transferred to your uterus using a thin, flexible catheter in a simple, painless procedure.

Two-Week Wait

Rest and recovery period while the embryo implants. We provide support and guidance during this important waiting period.

Pregnancy Test & Follow-up

Blood test to confirm pregnancy, followed by ongoing monitoring and support throughout your pregnancy journey.

Advantages of IVF

  • High Success Rates

    IVF offers some of the highest success rates among fertility treatments, especially for women under 35, with success rates continuing to improve with advancing technology.

  • Genetic Testing Options

    Pre-implantation genetic testing (PGT) can be performed to screen embryos for genetic disorders, increasing the chances of a healthy pregnancy.

  • Helps Various Conditions

    Effective treatment for blocked fallopian tubes, severe male factor infertility, endometriosis, unexplained infertility, and advanced maternal age.

  • Fertility Preservation

    Allows freezing of eggs, sperm, or embryos for future use, providing options for cancer patients or those wanting to delay childbearing.

  • Advanced Laboratory Control

    Fertilization occurs in a controlled laboratory environment, allowing for optimal conditions and close monitoring of embryo development.

  • Timing Control

    Provides better control over timing of conception, allowing couples to plan around work, health conditions, or other life circumstances.

  • Multiple Pregnancy Options

    Frozen embryo transfers allow for multiple pregnancy attempts from a single IVF cycle, reducing the need for repeated ovarian stimulation.

  • Emotional Support

    Comprehensive care teams provide psychological support and counseling throughout the process, helping couples navigate the emotional journey.
