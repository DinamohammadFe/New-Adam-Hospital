What is Reproductive Immunology?

Reproductive immunology is a specialized field that studies how the immune system affects fertility and pregnancy. The immune system normally protects the body from foreign substances, but during pregnancy, it must allow the embryo (which contains genetic material from both parents) to implant and develop.

When the immune system is overactive or misdirected, it can interfere with conception, implantation, or pregnancy maintenance, leading to infertility, recurrent pregnancy loss, or repeated IVF failures.

Conditions We Treat

Recurrent Pregnancy Loss

Multiple miscarriages that may be caused by immune factors attacking the developing pregnancy.

Repeated IVF Failure

Failed embryo implantation despite good quality embryos and optimal conditions.

Autoimmune Conditions

Conditions like antiphospholipid syndrome, thyroid disorders, and other autoimmune diseases affecting fertility.

Natural Killer Cell Issues

Elevated or abnormally functioning natural killer cells that may interfere with implantation.

Comprehensive Testing Options

Autoantibody Testing

Testing for antibodies that may attack reproductive tissues or interfere with pregnancy.

Natural Killer Cell Analysis

Measuring NK cell levels and activity to assess immune function.

Cytokine Profiling

Analyzing inflammatory markers and immune system balance.

Thrombophilia Screening

Testing for blood clotting disorders that can affect pregnancy.

Endometrial Receptivity

Assessing the uterine lining's readiness for embryo implantation.

HLA Typing

Human leukocyte antigen testing for compatibility assessment.

Treatment Approaches

Immunosuppressive Therapy

Medications to modulate immune system activity and reduce inflammation.

Immunoglobulin Therapy

Intravenous immunoglobulin (IVIG) to regulate immune responses.

Anticoagulation

Blood thinning medications for clotting disorders affecting pregnancy.

Natural Approaches

Nutritional supplements and lifestyle modifications to support immune balance.

Specialized Protocols

Customized IVF protocols designed for immune-related fertility issues.

Monitoring & Support

Ongoing monitoring and adjustment of treatments based on response.

When to Consider Reproductive Immunology

Recurrent Losses

Two or more consecutive pregnancy losses, especially if no other cause is found.

Multiple IVF Failures

Three or more failed IVF cycles with good quality embryos.

Autoimmune History

Personal or family history of autoimmune conditions.

Unexplained Infertility

Infertility with no identifiable cause after standard testing.
