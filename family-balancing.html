What is Family Balancing?

Family balancing, also known as gender selection or sex selection, is a reproductive technology that allows parents to choose the gender of their baby. This is achieved through Preimplantation Genetic Testing (PGT) during an IVF cycle, where embryos are tested for their chromosomal composition before transfer to the uterus.

The process involves creating embryos through IVF, testing them for gender (and genetic health), and then selecting embryos of the desired gender for transfer. This technology offers over 99% accuracy in gender selection.

How Family Balancing Works

Step 1: IVF Cycle

The process begins with a standard IVF cycle, including ovarian stimulation, egg retrieval, and fertilization in the laboratory.

Step 2: Embryo Development

Embryos are cultured in the laboratory for 5-6 days until they reach the blastocyst stage for optimal testing.

Step 3: Genetic Testing

A few cells are removed from each embryo and tested for chromosomal composition, including gender determination.

Step 4: Embryo Transfer

Healthy embryos of the desired gender are selected for transfer to the uterus, with remaining embryos frozen for future use.

Important Considerations

Legal & Ethical

Family balancing is legal in many countries but regulations vary. We ensure all procedures comply with local laws and ethical guidelines.

Cost Considerations

Family balancing requires IVF with PGT, which involves additional costs compared to standard fertility treatments.

Time Investment

The process typically takes 2-3 months from start to embryo transfer, with additional time for genetic testing results.

Emotional Support

Our counseling team provides emotional support throughout the process to help you make informed decisions.

Why Choose Family Balancing?

Family Completion

Couples who have children of one gender and desire a child of the opposite gender to complete their family.

Genetic Reasons

Preventing transmission of sex-linked genetic disorders that affect only one gender.

Personal Choice

Personal, cultural, or social reasons for preferring a child of a specific gender.
