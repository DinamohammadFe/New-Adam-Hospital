What is Embryo Freezing?

Embryo freezing is a process where embryos created during an IVF cycle are frozen for future use. After eggs are fertilized with sperm in the Assisted Reproduction laboratory, the resulting embryos are carefully monitored as they develop in the incubator. Embryos that are not transferred to the patient's uterus can be frozen using a technique called vitrification. This process preserves their quality, enabling their use in a future pregnancy.

Advantages of Embryo Freezing

Embryo freezing provides a crucial advantage for those undergoing IVF treatment. It offers flexibility, maximizes the chances of a successful pregnancy, and provides peace of mind for the future.

CREATING MULTIPLE HEALTHY EMBRYOS IN ONE IVF CYCLE
+

The goal of IVF is to create several healthy embryos in a single treatment cycle. At Adam, we recommend transferring only one embryo - or, in rare cases, two - to minimize the risk of multiple pregnancies. Any healthy embryos not transferred during the initial cycle are frozen for future use.

This approach increases the chances of achieving pregnancy in a single cycle. It saves time and resources and reduces the need for repeated treatments.

ENSURING HEALTHY EMBRYOS WITH GENETIC TESTING
+

Embryo freezing is essential when performing genetic testing. Preimplantation Genetic Testing (PGT) screens embryos for potential genetic abnormalities before transfer.

Embryos undergo a biopsy for genetic testing, after which they are frozen while awaiting test results. Once the testing is complete, the healthiest embryo is selected for thawing and transfer, increasing the likelihood of a healthy pregnancy.

STORING EMBRYOS FOR FUTURE USE
+

Embryo freezing allows for the storage of healthy embryos for future pregnancies. Whether the first transfer does not result in a pregnancy or you wish to expand your family later, frozen embryos can be thawed and transferred when you are ready.

With frozen embryos, there is no need to undergo a new IVF cycle. At Adam, we use state-of-the-art vitrification methods to ensure your embryos are stored safely, maintaining their viability and health.

Did You Know?

Embryos can be safely stored for over 10 years using advanced vitrification technology, with survival rates exceeding 95% upon thawing, giving you the flexibility to plan your family on your timeline?

Embryo Freezing Step by Step

Your embryo freezing journey is carefully planned and executed with precision. Here's what the process typically involves, so you can feel confident about preserving your fertility options.

Initial Consultation

Meet with our fertility specialist to discuss your goals, medical history, and determine if embryo freezing is right for you. We'll create a personalized plan tailored to your needs.

Embryo Creation

If you don't already have embryos, we'll guide you through the IVF process to create high-quality embryos. This includes ovarian stimulation, egg retrieval, and fertilization in our advanced laboratory.

Embryo Assessment

Our embryologists carefully evaluate each embryo for quality and developmental potential. Only the highest-quality embryos are selected for freezing to maximize future success rates.

Vitrification Process

Using advanced vitrification technology, embryos are rapidly frozen to -196Â°C in liquid nitrogen. This ultra-fast freezing prevents ice crystal formation and preserves cellular integrity.

Secure Storage

Your frozen embryos are stored in our state-of-the-art cryopreservation facility with 24/7 monitoring, backup systems, and comprehensive security measures to ensure their safety.

Future Use

When you're ready to use your frozen embryos, they'll be carefully thawed and transferred to your uterus. Our high survival rates and advanced techniques give you the best chance of achieving pregnancy when the time is right for you.

Success Rates with Frozen Embryos

High Success Rates

Frozen embryo transfer (FET) cycles have success rates comparable to fresh embryo transfers, with pregnancy rates of 40-60% per transfer depending on patient age and embryo quality.

Quality Preservation

Our advanced vitrification technology ensures over 95% embryo survival rate after thawing, maintaining the same genetic potential as fresh embryos.

Factors Affecting Success Rates

Success rates depend on various factors including maternal age at the time of egg retrieval, embryo quality and stage at freezing, endometrial preparation, and overall reproductive health. Our experienced team will discuss your individual prognosis during consultation.
