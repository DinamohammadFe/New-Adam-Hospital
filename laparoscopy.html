What is Laparoscopy?

Laparoscopy, also known as "keyhole surgery," is a minimally invasive surgical technique that uses a thin, lighted camera (laparoscope) inserted through small incisions in the abdomen. This allows surgeons to view and operate on internal organs with precision while minimizing trauma to surrounding tissues.

In fertility medicine, laparoscopy serves both diagnostic and therapeutic purposes, helping identify causes of infertility and treating conditions that may prevent conception. The procedure offers superior visualization of pelvic organs and allows for precise surgical interventions.

Types of Laparoscopic Procedures

Diagnostic Laparoscopy

  • Evaluate unexplained infertility
  • Assess pelvic pain causes
  • Examine fallopian tubes
  • Check for endometriosis
  • Investigate ovarian cysts
  • Assess pelvic adhesions

Operative Laparoscopy

  • Remove endometriosis
  • Treat ovarian cysts
  • Remove fibroids
  • Release adhesions
  • Tubal surgery
  • Ovarian drilling

Conditions Treated with Laparoscopy

Endometriosis

Removal of endometrial tissue growing outside the uterus that can cause infertility.

Ovarian Cysts

Treatment of cysts that may interfere with ovulation or fertility.

Pelvic Adhesions

Removal of scar tissue that can block fallopian tubes or affect organ function.

Tubal Blockages

Opening blocked fallopian tubes to restore natural fertility.

Uterine Fibroids

Removal of fibroids that may impact implantation or pregnancy.

PCOS Treatment

Ovarian drilling to improve ovulation in polycystic ovary syndrome.

The Laparoscopic Process

Pre-operative Preparation

Comprehensive evaluation, blood tests, and pre-surgical instructions.

Minimally Invasive Surgery

Small incisions (5-10mm) with camera and specialized instruments.

Real-time Visualization

High-definition camera provides clear view of pelvic organs.

Same-day Discharge

Most patients can go home the same day with minimal recovery time.

  • Reduced risk of infection
  • Same-day or overnight stay
  • Superior visualization
  • Precise surgical control
  • Lower adhesion formation
