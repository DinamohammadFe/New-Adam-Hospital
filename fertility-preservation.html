Frequently Asked Questions

The ideal age for egg freezing is typically before age 35, when egg quality and quantity are at their peak. However, we can assess your individual situation at any age.

Frozen eggs can be stored indefinitely. There is no evidence of deterioration in egg quality over time when properly frozen and stored.

Success rates depend on age at freezing and number of eggs stored. Generally, women under 35 have the highest success rates, with 85-95% egg survival after thawing.

Coverage varies by insurance plan and indication. Medical fertility preservation (before cancer treatment) is often covered, while elective preservation may not be. We'll help verify your coverage.
