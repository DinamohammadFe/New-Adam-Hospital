What is Hysteroscopy?

Hysteroscopy is a minimally invasive procedure that uses a thin, lighted telescope called a hysteroscope to examine the inside of the uterus (uterine cavity). The hysteroscope is inserted through the vagina and cervix, allowing direct visualization of the uterine lining and detection of any abnormalities.

This procedure can be both diagnostic (to identify problems) and operative (to treat conditions). It provides superior visualization compared to other imaging methods and allows for immediate treatment of many uterine conditions that can affect fertility.

Types of Hysteroscopic Procedures

Diagnostic Hysteroscopy

  • Evaluate abnormal bleeding
  • Investigate infertility causes
  • Assess recurrent miscarriages
  • Examine uterine shape
  • Check for polyps or fibroids
  • Evaluate adhesions

Operative Hysteroscopy

  • Remove uterine polyps
  • Treat submucosal fibroids
  • Divide uterine septum
  • Remove intrauterine adhesions
  • Endometrial ablation
  • Foreign body removal

Conditions Treated with Hysteroscopy

Uterine Polyps

Benign growths in the uterine lining that can affect implantation and fertility.

Submucosal Fibroids

Fibroids that grow into the uterine cavity and can impact pregnancy.

Uterine Septum

Congenital condition where tissue divides the uterine cavity.

Intrauterine Adhesions

Scar tissue inside the uterus that can prevent implantation.

Abnormal Bleeding

Heavy or irregular menstrual bleeding affecting quality of life.

Recurrent Miscarriage

Investigating uterine causes of pregnancy loss.
