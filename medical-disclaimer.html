3. Treatment Risks and Limitations

All medical treatments, including fertility treatments, carry inherent risks and potential complications. It is important to understand these risks before proceeding with any treatment.

Common Fertility Treatment Risks May Include:
  • Multiple pregnancy (twins, triplets, or higher-order multiples)
  • Ovarian hyperstimulation syndrome (OHSS)
  • Ectopic pregnancy
  • Miscarriage
  • Birth defects (though rates are generally similar to natural conception)
  • Emotional and psychological stress
  • Financial burden
  • Side effects from medications
  • Complications from procedures
  • Infection

Treatment Limitations

Fertility treatments do not guarantee pregnancy or live birth. Success depends on numerous factors including age, underlying medical conditions, response to treatment, and other variables that may be beyond medical control.

4. Success Rates and Outcomes

Important Notes About Success Rates:
  • Published success rates are based on historical data and population averages
  • Your individual chances may be higher or lower than published rates
  • Success rates vary significantly based on age, diagnosis, and other factors
  • Past performance does not guarantee future results
  • Success rates may change over time as techniques and protocols evolve

Factors Affecting Success

Multiple factors can influence treatment outcomes, including:

  • Age of the female partner
  • Cause and duration of infertility
  • Previous pregnancy history
  • Lifestyle factors (smoking, weight, stress)
  • Response to fertility medications
  • Quality of eggs and sperm
  • Uterine and tubal factors
  • Overall health status

5. Individual Results May Vary

Every patient is unique, and treatment outcomes can vary significantly from person to person. Factors that may influence your individual results include your medical history, age, lifestyle, adherence to treatment protocols, and other variables.

Why Results Vary

  • Genetic factors unique to each individual
  • Underlying medical conditions
  • Response to medications and treatments
  • Lifestyle and environmental factors
  • Psychological and emotional factors
  • Partner factors in cases of couple infertility

We cannot predict with certainty how you will respond to treatment or what your specific outcomes will be. Your medical team will work with you to optimize your treatment plan based on your individual circumstances.

6. Informed Consent

Before beginning any treatment, you will be provided with detailed information about the proposed procedures, including risks, benefits, alternatives, and expected outcomes. You have the right to ask questions and seek clarification about any aspect of your treatment.

Your Rights Include:

  • The right to receive complete information about your condition and treatment options
  • The right to ask questions and receive clear answers
  • The right to seek a second opinion
  • The right to refuse or discontinue treatment
  • The right to participate in treatment decisions
  • The right to privacy and confidentiality

Informed consent is an ongoing process, and you may ask questions or express concerns at any time during your treatment.

7. Second Opinions

We encourage patients to seek second opinions when making important medical decisions. Getting a second opinion can provide additional perspective and help ensure you are making the best decision for your situation.

When to Consider a Second Opinion:

  • Before beginning a new treatment protocol
  • If you have concerns about your current treatment plan
  • When facing complex medical decisions
  • If you are not responding to treatment as expected
  • Before undergoing surgical procedures

We will provide you with copies of your medical records and test results to facilitate obtaining a second opinion from another qualified healthcare provider.

8. Emergency Care

Medical Emergencies

For life-threatening emergencies, call 911 immediately. Do not delay emergency care to contact our office.

When to Seek Emergency Care:

  • Severe abdominal pain
  • Heavy vaginal bleeding
  • Signs of severe ovarian hyperstimulation syndrome
  • Severe allergic reactions
  • Chest pain or difficulty breathing
  • Signs of ectopic pregnancy
  • Any condition that seems life-threatening

After-Hours Care

For non-emergency concerns outside of business hours, please call our main number and follow the prompts for after-hours care. A healthcare provider will return your call as soon as possible.
