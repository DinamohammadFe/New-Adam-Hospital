What is Myomectomy?

Myomectomy is a surgical procedure designed to remove uterine fibroids (leiomyomas) while preserving the uterus. Unlike hysterectomy, which removes the entire uterus, myomectomy allows women to maintain their reproductive capacity and is often the preferred treatment for women who wish to preserve fertility.

Fibroids are non-cancerous growths that develop in or around the uterus. They can cause heavy menstrual bleeding, pelvic pain, pressure symptoms, and fertility problems. Myomectomy effectively removes these problematic fibroids while leaving healthy uterine tissue intact, allowing for future pregnancies.

Understanding Uterine Fibroids

What are Fibroids?

  • Non-cancerous muscle tumors
  • Affect 20-80% of women
  • Hormone-dependent growth
  • Variable sizes and locations
  • Can impact fertility
  • Often multiple fibroids present

Fibroid Symptoms

  • Heavy menstrual bleeding
  • Prolonged periods
  • Pelvic pain and pressure
  • Frequent urination
  • Difficulty getting pregnant
  • Pregnancy complications

Types of Myomectomy Procedures

Hysteroscopic Myomectomy

Best for: Submucosal fibroids (inside uterine cavity)

Approach: Through the cervix, no incisions

Recovery: 1-2 days

Benefits: Minimally invasive, quick recovery

Laparoscopic Myomectomy

Best for: Subserosal and intramural fibroids

Approach: Small abdominal incisions

Recovery: 1-2 weeks

Benefits: Minimal scarring, faster healing

Abdominal Myomectomy

Best for: Large or multiple fibroids

Approach: Open abdominal surgery

Recovery: 4-6 weeks

Benefits: Complete fibroid removal

Who Needs Myomectomy?

Fertility Preservation

Women who want to maintain their ability to have children.

Heavy Bleeding

Severe menstrual bleeding affecting quality of life.

Infertility Issues

Fibroids interfering with conception or implantation.

Pregnancy Complications

Recurrent miscarriages or pregnancy complications.

Pressure Symptoms

Pelvic pressure, frequent urination, or bowel problems.

Failed Medical Treatment

Symptoms not controlled with medication.

Fibroid Types by Location

Submucosal Fibroids

Grow into the uterine cavity, cause heavy bleeding and fertility issues.

Intramural Fibroids

Grow within the uterine wall, most common type, cause enlargement.

Subserosal Fibroids

Grow outward from uterus, cause pressure and bulk symptoms.
