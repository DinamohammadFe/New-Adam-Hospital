What is IVF-ICSI?

IVF-ICSI increases fertilization chances, even with severe male infertility. It enables the use of very few or surgically retrieved sperm, supports genetic testing, and improves overall success rates for couples facing complex fertility challenges.

IVF-ICSI Step by Step

Ovarian Stimulation

Daily hormone injections (FSH and LH) are administered for 8-14 days to stimulate the ovaries to develop multiple follicles containing mature eggs. Regular monitoring through blood tests and ultrasounds tracks follicle growth and hormone levels to determine the optimal timing for egg retrieval.

Egg Retrieval

A minimally invasive 20-30 minute procedure performed under light sedation. Using transvaginal ultrasound guidance, a thin needle aspirates mature eggs from the follicles. The procedure is typically painless with minimal recovery time, and patients can return home the same day.

Sperm Collection / Retrieval

Fresh sperm sample is collected on the same day as egg retrieval through masturbation in a private room. For men with severe male factor infertility, surgical sperm retrieval (TESE, PESA, or MESA) may be performed to extract sperm directly from the testicles or epididymis under local anesthesia.

ICSI Procedure

Using advanced micromanipulation techniques under high-powered microscopy, embryologists carefully select the best quality sperm and inject it directly into the cytoplasm of each mature egg using a glass micropipette. This precise procedure bypasses natural fertilization barriers and significantly increases fertilization rates, especially for severe male factor infertility.

Embryo Culture

Fertilized embryos are cultured in specialized incubators that maintain optimal temperature, humidity, and CO2 levels. Embryologists monitor development daily, assessing cell division, morphology, and growth patterns. Embryos are typically cultured for 3-5 days until they reach the blastocyst stage, when they have the highest implantation potential.

Embryo Transfer

A simple, painless procedure where the highest quality embryo(s) are transferred into the uterus using a thin, flexible catheter guided by ultrasound. The number of embryos transferred depends on age, embryo quality, and previous cycle history. Single embryo transfer is often recommended to reduce multiple pregnancy risks while maintaining high success rates.

Pregnancy Test

Approximately 10-14 days after embryo transfer, a blood test measures beta-hCG hormone levels to confirm pregnancy. If positive, follow-up blood tests monitor rising hormone levels, and an ultrasound scan at 6-7 weeks confirms fetal heartbeat and proper implantation. Progesterone support continues through the first trimester to maintain pregnancy.

IVF Success Rates

High Success Rates

ICSI treatment achieves successful fertilization in between 50-80 per cent of cases . The rest is down to the subsequent IVF treatment and the successful implantation of the embryo into the womb lining after being transferred.

Quality Preservation

Advanced lab techniques ensure eggs and embryos are carefully handled, frozen, and stored. This maintains their viability, giving patients the best chance for future healthy pregnancies.

Factors Affecting Success Rates

Success rates depend on various factors including maternal age at the time of egg retrieval, embryo quality and stage at freezing, endometrial preparation, and overall reproductive health. Our experienced team will discuss your individual prognosis during consultation.
