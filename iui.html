IUI Treatment

Intrauterine Insemination (IUI) is a fertility treatment that involves placing sperm directly into a woman's uterus to facilitate fertilization. This less invasive procedure can be an effective first step in fertility treatment.

IUI is often recommended as a first-line treatment for couples with unexplained infertility, mild male factor infertility, or cervical factor infertility. It's a simpler and less expensive alternative to IVF.

What is IUI?

Intrauterine Insemination (IUI) is a fertility treatment where specially prepared sperm is placed directly into the uterus around the time of ovulation. This procedure bypasses the cervix and places sperm closer to the fallopian tubes, increasing the chances of fertilization. IUI is often combined with fertility medications to stimulate ovulation and increase the number of eggs released.

The procedure is relatively simple and can be performed in a doctor's office. It's less invasive and less expensive than IVF, making it an attractive first option for many couples experiencing fertility challenges.

The IUI Process

Step 1: Monitoring

Your menstrual cycle is monitored through blood tests and ultrasounds to determine the optimal timing for insemination.

Step 2: Medication (Optional)

Fertility medications may be prescribed to stimulate ovulation and increase the number of eggs released.

Step 3: Sperm Preparation

Sperm is collected and processed in the laboratory to concentrate the healthiest, most motile sperm.

Step 4: Insemination

The prepared sperm is inserted directly into the uterus using a thin, flexible catheter during a quick, painless procedure.

Success rates vary based on age, underlying fertility issues, and whether fertility medications are used. Most couples try 3-6 cycles of IUI before considering other treatments like IVF.

Who Benefits from IUI?

Unexplained Infertility

Couples who have been trying to conceive for over a year with no identifiable cause of infertility.

Mild Male Factor

Men with slightly low sperm count, motility, or morphology that may benefit from sperm concentration.

Cervical Issues

Women with cervical mucus problems or cervical scarring that may impede sperm passage.

Ovulation Disorders

Women with irregular ovulation who can benefit from fertility medications combined with IUI.
