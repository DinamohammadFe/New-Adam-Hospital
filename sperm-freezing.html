What is Sperm Freezing?

Sperm freezing, also known as sperm cryopreservation, is a process where sperm samples are collected, processed, and stored at extremely low temperatures for future use. This fertility preservation technique allows men to maintain their reproductive options by preserving sperm for later use in assisted reproductive treatments. The frozen sperm can be thawed and used for intrauterine insemination (IUI) or in vitro fertilization (IVF) when ready to conceive.

Benefits of Sperm Freezing

Sperm freezing provides men with reproductive security and peace of mind. It offers protection against future fertility challenges and ensures genetic material is preserved for family planning.

MEDICAL FERTILITY PRESERVATION
+

For men facing cancer treatments, surgery, or other medical procedures that may affect fertility, sperm freezing offers hope for future biological children. It's essential to preserve sperm before starting treatments that could impact sperm production.

Our team works closely with oncologists and other specialists to provide timely fertility preservation, ensuring you have the best chance of preserving your reproductive potential before treatment begins.

CAREER AND LIFESTYLE PLANNING
+

Men in high-risk occupations, military service, or those planning extended travel can preserve their fertility before deployment or exposure to potentially harmful environments. This provides security for future family planning.

Sperm freezing also benefits men who want to delay fatherhood while pursuing career goals or those concerned about age-related decline in sperm quality over time.

BACKUP FOR FERTILITY TREATMENTS
+

Sperm freezing provides a backup option for couples undergoing IVF or other fertility treatments. It ensures sperm availability on the day of egg retrieval, reducing stress and eliminating the risk of collection difficulties.

This is particularly valuable for men with performance anxiety, irregular schedules, or those who may be traveling during treatment cycles, ensuring treatment can proceed as planned.

Did You Know?

Sperm can be successfully frozen and stored for decades without significant loss of quality? Many men have successfully fathered children using sperm that was frozen for 10+ years, making it one of the most reliable fertility preservation methods available?

Sperm Freezing Step by Step

Your sperm freezing journey is straightforward and confidential. Here's what the process typically involves, ensuring your comfort and privacy throughout.

Initial Consultation

Meet with our fertility specialist to discuss your goals, medical history, and reasons for sperm freezing. We'll explain the process, answer your questions, and create a personalized plan.

Health Screening

Complete health screening including blood tests for infectious diseases (HIV, Hepatitis B & C, syphilis) as required by regulations. This ensures the safety of stored samples and future use.

Sample Collection

Provide sperm samples in our private, comfortable collection rooms. Multiple samples may be collected over several visits to ensure adequate storage for future use.

Sperm Analysis

Our laboratory analyzes each sample for count, motility, and morphology. This assessment helps determine the best freezing protocol and provides information about future treatment options.

Cryopreservation Process

Sperm is mixed with cryoprotectant solution and gradually frozen to -196Â°C in liquid nitrogen. This controlled freezing process preserves sperm viability and function for future use.

Secure Storage

Your frozen sperm is stored in our state-of-the-art cryopreservation facility with 24/7 monitoring, backup systems, and comprehensive security measures to ensure long-term viability.

Future Use

When you're ready to use your frozen sperm, it will be carefully thawed and used for IUI or IVF procedures. Our high post-thaw survival rates ensure excellent chances of successful fertilization when the time is right.

Success Rates with Frozen Sperm

Excellent Survival Rates

Frozen sperm typically maintains 50-80% motility after thawing, with fertilization rates comparable to fresh sperm when used in IVF procedures.

Long-term Viability

Sperm can be stored indefinitely without deterioration in quality. Successful pregnancies have been achieved with sperm stored for over 20 years.

Factors Affecting Success

Success rates depend on sperm quality before freezing, the freezing and thawing process, and the type of fertility treatment used. Our experienced team will discuss your individual prognosis and treatment options.
