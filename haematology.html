How Blood Health Affects Fertility

Blood disorders can impact fertility in multiple ways. Clotting disorders may affect implantation and increase miscarriage risk, while anemia can reduce oxygen delivery to reproductive organs. Autoimmune blood conditions may interfere with conception and pregnancy maintenance.

Our integrated approach ensures that blood-related factors are properly evaluated and managed before and during fertility treatments, improving your chances of successful conception and healthy pregnancy outcomes.

Common Blood Conditions We Treat

Thrombophilia

Inherited or acquired tendency to form blood clots, which can affect implantation and pregnancy.

Iron Deficiency Anemia

Low iron levels that can affect egg quality, ovulation, and overall reproductive health.

Antiphospholipid Syndrome

Autoimmune condition causing blood clots and recurrent pregnancy loss.

Factor V Leiden

Genetic mutation increasing blood clot risk during pregnancy and fertility treatments.

Platelet Disorders

Conditions affecting blood clotting and bleeding risk during procedures and pregnancy.

Hemoglobinopathies

Inherited blood disorders like sickle cell disease and thalassemia affecting pregnancy.

When to Consult Our Haematology Team

Recurrent Pregnancy Loss

Multiple miscarriages that may be related to blood clotting disorders.

Family History

Known blood disorders or clotting problems in your family.

Previous Blood Clots

Personal history of deep vein thrombosis, pulmonary embolism, or stroke.

Unexplained Fatigue

Persistent tiredness that may indicate anemia or other blood disorders.

Abnormal Blood Tests

Unusual results in routine blood work that need specialist evaluation.

Pre-Treatment Screening

Routine evaluation before starting fertility treatments or pregnancy.
